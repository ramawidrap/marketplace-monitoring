## Coverage

- **Tokopedia — fully succeeded.** `WebFetch` on `/find/<slug>` SEO pages worked reliably across all combinations tried: `macbook-pro-m4-pro` and `macbook-air-m4` (both base slugs, mostly returned accessories at top when sorted by price — as expected per known method notes), `macbook-pro-m4-pro-second` and `macbook-air-m4-second` (`?ob=3`, cheapest-first — these surfaced real laptop listings), and `condition=2&ob=9` (newest used) on both base slugs. `condition=1&ob=3` (new, cheapest-first) returned only accessories for both products, consistent with new-unit retail prices being well above both thresholds. Individual product pages were fetched to verify chip identity for 4 listings (2 confirmed base-M4 exclusions, 2 confirmed genuine matches).
- **Shopee Indonesia — blocked, as before.** `curl` to the search page returned HTTP 200 but only the client-side-rendered JS shell (no product data in the static HTML). The internal `search_items` API returned HTTP 403 with anti-bot payload `error:90309999`, identical to the previous run. `WebFetch` on the search page returned an empty body. `WebSearch` successfully surfaced 10 real individual product URLs per product query (listed above as unverified candidates) — discovery works, direct verification does not.
- **Carousell (id.carousell.com) — blocked, as before.** Both `WebFetch` and `curl` returned HTTP 403 on search/category pages (`/q/`) and were not retried on individual `/p/` pages this run since no genuine candidate URLs were surfaced by search. `WebSearch` this run returned mostly irrelevant/older results for both products — a change from the previous run, which had found 3 genuine-looking candidate URLs via the same method. This may just be query-phrasing/index variance rather than a real change in Carousell's catalog; worth retrying with different query phrasing next run.
- No prices were fabricated or taken from search-snippet text alone. All reported prices (matches and reference listings) came from directly fetching a Tokopedia page. The two threshold matches were additionally cross-checked by fetching their individual product pages to confirm chip identity.

**Bottom line:** Tokopedia discovery and verification both worked well this run and, for the first time, turned up genuine listings under both thresholds. The `-second` slug variants (rather than the base slug) were essential — the base slugs are dominated by accessories even at `ob=3`. Careful chip verification mattered: several of the cheapest "M4 Pro"-search results and one "M4"-Air result were confirmed look-alikes (base M4 chip, or a mispriced multi-SKU listing) that would have been false positives if taken at face value. Shopee and Carousell remain unauditable beyond WebSearch-discovered candidate URLs.
